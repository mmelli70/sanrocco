Places to Visit
The garden on Lake Maggiore
Verbania is known as "il giardino sul Lago Maggiore" — a city of historic villas, world-class botanical parks and a spectacular lakefront promenade. It comprises 12 hamlets; the main ones for visitors are Pallanza and Intra.
In Verbania
Botanical Garden Must-See
Giardini Botanici di Villa Taranto
One of the most celebrated botanical gardens in the world — 16 hectares with over 20,000 plant varieties and 80,000 flowering bulbs. Founded in 1931 by Scottish captain Neil McEacharn. The blooms are extraordinary: tulips in spring, lotus flowers in summer, dahlias from July to October. An absolute highlight of any visit to Verbania.
📍Via Vittorio Veneto · Pallanza (lakefront)
🕐Open April–October · 8:30–18:30
💶€13 gardens / €15 full · villataranto.it
Open in Maps
Lakefront Promenade
Lungolago di Pallanza
The jewel of Verbania — a scenic waterfront promenade lined with magnolias, oleanders and historic villas. Villa Giulia (neoclassical, public garden) and Villa San Remigio (baroque, 19th century, private but visitable externally) are both worth admiring. The panorama across the Borromean Gulf is unforgettable. The full Pallanza–Suna walk is about 3–4 km.
📍Pallanza waterfront · Always open · Free
Open in Maps
Museum · Art & Culture
Museo del Paesaggio
Housed in the baroque Palazzo Viani-Dugnani in Pallanza, this museum holds the most important art collection on Lake Maggiore — 19th–20th century Piemontese and Lombard paintings, plus the sculptural works of Paolo Troubetzkoy. Visiting here helps make sense of the art you will notice throughout the city.
📍Via Ruga, 44 · Pallanza
🕐Tue–Sun 10:00–18:00 (seasonal)
Open in Maps
Church · National Monument
Chiesa della Madonna di Campagna
A national monument and one of the finest Renaissance churches on Lake Maggiore, built over an older Romanesque site — the original bell tower is the only visible remnant. Inside are masterpieces by Camillo Procaccini, Aurelio Luini and other Lombard painters.
📍Pallanza · near Villa Taranto · Free
Open in Maps
Church · Baroque · Intra
Basilica di San Vittore
The iconic 18th-century landmark of Intra, visible from the lake and declared a national monument. Built on an ancient proto-Christian site. Inside: a notable organ and paintings by Daniele Ranzoni. Eight bells in the campanile cast by the renowned Mazzola foundry.
📍Intra town centre · Free
Open in Maps
Nature Reserve · Birdwatching
Riserva Naturale di Fondotoce
A peaceful nature reserve at the mouth of the River Toce, between Pallanza and Intra. Reedbeds and over 120 bird species. Walking and cycling paths wind through the reserve — a beautiful escape from the town, especially early in the morning.
📍Fondotoce · Free · Always open

Day Trips
Day Trip Iconic
Isole Borromee
The three Borromean Islands are the crown jewels of Lake Maggiore. Isola Bella: baroque Palazzo Borromeo and spectacular terraced Italian gardens. Isola Madre: lush botanical garden with exotic plants, peacocks and parrots. Isola dei Pescatori: picturesque fishing village — no admission fee, open year-round. Ferries depart from the Intra and Pallanza piers.
⛴️Navigazione Laghi · navigazionelaghi.it
📍Depart from Intra (Vecchio Imbarcadero) or Pallanza pier
Open in Maps
Day Trip · Adventure
Lago Maggiore Zipline
A thrilling 1,850-metre steel cable running at 350 metres above the lake at over 120 km/h. Departing from Pian d'Arla and arriving at Alpe Segletta in the Valle Intrasca above Verbania. One of the most spectacular ziplines in Europe.
📍Valle Intrasca · above Verbania
💶Booking required · seasonal operation
Day Trip · Mountain viewpoint
Mottarone (1,491 m)
A spectacular peak between Lake Maggiore and Lake Orta, offering one of the finest panoramic views in Northern Italy. Take the Stresa–Mottarone cable car (from Stresa, ~30 min drive south). Walking and cycling in summer; ski slopes in winter. The Alpyland alpine coaster is popular with families.
📍Cable car from Stresa · ~30 min by car
🌐stresa-mottarone.it
Day Trip · Northern Lake
Cannobio
A charming village near the Swiss border, ~30 km north of Verbania. Its beautiful tree-lined lungolago is ideal for a romantic evening walk. The surrounding valleys offer excellent hiking, including the dramatic Orrido di Sant'Anna gorge. Good for water sports in summer.
📍~30 km north of Verbania via SS34

Verbania is also an excellent base for day trips into Switzerland.

Transport
Getting around Verbania & the lake
By Bus
Local & Regional Bus
VCO Trasporti
The main public bus operator covering Verbania and the province. Urban lines connect Intra, Pallanza, Fondotoce and the train station (every ~30 min). Line 3 runs north to Cannobio and into Switzerland (Brissago). Tickets and timetables at vcotrasporti.it. Freephone: 800 503001.
🌐vcotrasporti.it
📞+39 0323 518611 · Freephone 800 503001
Bus · South Lake & Airport
Autoservizi SAF / Nerini
SAF (Autoservizi Nerini) runs routes to Arona, Novara, Miazzina and Valle Intrasca. Also operates the Malpensa Airport Alibus shuttle — booking required by 11:00 the day before. Journey time to Malpensa: approx. 1 hour.
📞+39 0323 552172
🛫Malpensa shuttle — advance booking required
By Train
Railway Station
Stazione Verbania-Pallanza
Verbania's station is on the Milan–Domodossola international line (Trenitalia). Direct trains to Milan Centrale (approx. 1h 20 min). The station is in Fondotoce, 8 km from the town centre — VCO Trasporti buses connect every ~30 minutes. Taxis available at the station coinciding with train arrivals. Alternative route: Trenord to Laveno, then Laveno–Intra ferry.
📍Fondotoce · 8 km from Pallanza/Intra
🌐trenitalia.com
By Boat
Lake Ferries Highly Recommended
Navigazione Laghi
The public lake ferry is the most scenic way to explore Lake Maggiore. Scheduled routes connect Verbania (Intra and Pallanza piers) to the Borromean Islands, Stresa, Baveno and all lakeside towns up to Locarno, Switzerland. A vehicle car ferry also links Verbania Intra to Laveno on the Lombardy shore.
📍Intra — Vecchio Imbarcadero · and Pallanza pier
🌐navigazionelaghi.it
📞+39 0322 233230

Best Time to Visit
Seasons on Lake Maggiore
Spring (Apr–Jun): Ideal — tulips and blooms at Villa Taranto, mild temperatures, fewer crowds.
Summer (Jul–Aug): Warm and lively; perfect for boat trips, swimming and lakeside dining.
Autumn (Sep–Oct): Beautiful golden light, dahlias still in bloom, a quieter and more local atmosphere.
Winter: Very peaceful; many gardens and restaurants close seasonally.
